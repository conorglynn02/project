Design Ideas


Pattern

As you can see in the image at the top of the page, I have named some of my design ideas for this dash-cam concept. I chose the two patterns for the dash-cam's surface. One was the Arsenal 2020/21 home kit style while the other was the Arsenal 2020/21 away kit style. For reference, you can see both of these designs next to the actual product below. I used the home kit pattern for the two legs of the dash-cam while I used the away kit for the cone of the cannon. See below Arsenal jersey designs on left with actual dash-cam with designs on right. (Note: pattern on cone is hard to see because of strong artificial lighting in the render studio.)





Wheel Zoom

Even when considering a wheel's natural tendency to roll, I still wanted to include the wheel as it is a big part of Arsenal's crest and an 18th century cannon's functionality. I came up with the idea to have the wheel locked into place so it wouldn't roll off the dashboard or out of line of the road. After thinking about it for a little bit, I figured out a way to stop the rolling while also letting the wheel serve a purpose. I came to the conclusion of having the wheels decently resistant to any rotation so normal movement of the car wont spin the wheels in any way. However if a human tries to spin the wheel, it will click into the next groove and it will either zoom in or out depending on which way it was rotated. The centre of the wheel also is used as a power on/off switch too. See product wheel on left.



Solar Panels

This dash-cam doesn't need any wires because this dash-cam is completely powered by solar panels. By day, this dash-cam charges up its large lithium-ion battery to be used later. In the day it also uses the sun's energy to completely run the dash-cam while still charging the battery. Even when not in use, the dash-cam can recharge its battery to full capacity. By night, the lithium-ion battery kicks in and uses the sun's stored energy to run. This format makes the dash-cam run completely on clean, green, renewable energy which is completely free. I think there would be more public support for an idea that is environmentally friendly so would increase sales.




Sponsors

The main sponsors of Arsenal F.C. are Fly Emirates, Adidas and Visit Rwanda. These partners give massive amounts of financial funding to Arsenal F.C. while in exchange getting different types of perks. For Fly Emirates and Visit Rwanda, both of these pay tens of millions per year to be featured on the jersey. With Fly Emirates 'donating' more money than Visit Rwanda, they are the main sponsor of Arsenal. They appear on the front of the jersey and they are also given the name of Arsenal's stadium, Emirates Stadium. Visit Rwanda name is located on the sleeve of the jersey and also as sponsorship in the stadium. Adidas has a different type of sponsorship deal with Arsenal than the others. Adidas is the highest paid sponsor of Arsenal currently. On the other side of the coin, Adidas recieves the lion's share of the money spent on jersey sales. This makes Adidas profit for their investment and Arsenal gets a fraction of the profit.
